# Diffuse Midline Glioma / Brainstem Glioma — MDT Summary

Diffuse midline glioma, H3 K27-altered, is a serious pediatric CNS tumor category associated with midline structures such as pons, thalamus, spinal cord, medulla, and midbrain. Imaging and pathology should be interpreted together.

Surgical strategy is usually constrained by eloquent anatomy. Biopsy for molecular confirmation may be discussed when it can be performed safely. Maximal resection is not generally feasible for intrinsic pontine tumors and could carry substantial neurologic risk.

Molecular testing may include H3K27 alteration status, BRAF, IDH, ATRX, TP53, MGMT promoter methylation where relevant, and methylation profiling depending on institutional workflow.

Trial matching should consider age, tumor location, prior therapy, performance status, steroid requirements, measurable disease, and molecular markers.
